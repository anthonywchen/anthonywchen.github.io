Anthony Chen

Welcome! I'm a third-year PhD student working on machine learning and natural language processing at UC Irvine where I have the pleasure of being advised by Sameer Singh. I am fortunate to also work with Matt Gardner from AI2 and Gabi Stanovsky from Hebrew University.

My research is focused on the following topics:

Natural Language Generation: Much of NLP research is focused on classification, however, classification formats can permit easy heuristics. Posing tasks in a generation format alleviates some of these issues, but evaluation is challenging. We show that learned metrics are a better path towards NLG evaluation for QA.

Long Tail of Natural Language: Natural distributions of language have a long tail which isn't well represented in existing datasets. I'm interested in designing datasets that can represent the long tail and creating models that are robust on these long tail instances.
