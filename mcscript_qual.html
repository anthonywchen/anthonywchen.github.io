Read the instructions and examples, even if you have done similar tasks, as the examples may have changed.


Then answer the 10 questions, referring to the examples when necessary. You will need at least 7 correct to pass. You will be paid whether you pass or not.


If you pass, you will receive the "mcscript" qualification. The qualification will allow you to work on the 1,000 HITs for this task that we will release over time.


In this task, you are given a passage, a question, a correct answer, and a predicted answer.

The task is to score how closely a predicted answer captures the same information (i.e. have the same meaning) as a correct answer using the passage, question, and human commonsense knowledge.

Scoring is on a scale from 1 to 5 .
A 1 score means the predicted answer captures none of the information of the correct answer, and thus is a completely wrong answer.
A 5 score means the predicted answer captures the same information as the correct answer, and thus is a completely right answer.


Note: A predicted answer may answer the question correctly, but to be given a score of 5, it must be equivalent to the correct answer.

Note: The score you give should not depend on the capitalization of the correct and predicted answer.

Note: A helpful tip for searching for the correct answer in the passage is using Control+F to search for it.

(Completely Right) Predicted answers that should recieve a 5:

  Some ways a predicted answer can get a score of 5.
  • It is clear that that the predicted and correct answers refer to the same person, place, or object.
  • The predicted and correct answers are synonyms.
